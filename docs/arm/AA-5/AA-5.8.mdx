<MarginText>1</MarginText>
<p>[A <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code> specifies an explicit transfer of control from this <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0167">statement</a></code> to a target statement with a given label.] <br /></p>


#### Syntax

<MarginText>2</MarginText>

<CodeBlock>
<code>goto_statement</code><a id="S0194"></a><code> ::= </code><strong>goto</strong> <em>label_</em><code><a href="../AA-4/AA-4.1#S0091">name</a></code>;<br />

</CodeBlock>

#### Name Resolution Rules

<MarginText>3</MarginText>
<p>The <em>label_</em><code><a href="../AA-4/AA-4.1#S0091">name</a></code> shall resolve to denote a <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0171">label</a></code>; the <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0167">statement</a></code> with that <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0171">label</a></code> is the <em>target statement</em>. <br /></p>


#### Legality Rules

<MarginText>4</MarginText>
<p>The innermost <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0166">sequence_of_statements</a></code> that encloses the target statement shall also enclose the <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code>. Furthermore, if a <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code> is enclosed by an <code><a href="../AA-9/AA-9.5#S0258">accept_statement</a></code> or a body, then the target statement shall not be outside this enclosing construct. <br /></p>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>4.a</MarginText>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="ramification">
<strong></strong>The <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code> can be a <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0167">statement</a></code> of an inner <code>sequence_</code>.<br /></Admonition>
</AnnotatedOnly>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>4.b</MarginText>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note">
It follows from the second rule that if the target <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0167">statement</a></code> is enclosed by such a construct, then the <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code> cannot be outside. <br /></Admonition>
</AnnotatedOnly>


#### Dynamic Semantics

<MarginText>5</MarginText>
<p>The execution of a <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code> transfers control to the target statement, completing the execution of any <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0169">compound_statement</a></code> that encloses the <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.8#S0194">goto_statement</a></code> but does not enclose the target. <br /></p>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>6</MarginText>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note">
NOTE   The above rules allow transfer of control to a <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0167">statement</a></code> of an enclosing <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0166">sequence_of_statements</a></code> but not the reverse. Similarly, they prohibit transfers of control such as between alternatives of a <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.4#S0176">case_statement</a></code>, <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.3#S0175">if_statement</a></code>, or <code><a href="../AA-9/AA-9.7#S0269">select_statement</a></code>; between <code><a href="../AA-11/AA-11.2#S0305">exception_handler</a></code>s; or from an <code><a href="../AA-11/AA-11.2#S0305">exception_handler</a></code> of a <code><a href="../AA-11/AA-11.2#S0304">handled_sequence_of_statements</a></code> back to its <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0166">sequence_of_statements</a></code>. <br /></Admonition>
</AnnotatedOnly>

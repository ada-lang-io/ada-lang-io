<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
1
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<p>[In contrast to the entities declared in the visible part of a package, the entities declared in the <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> are visible only within the <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> itself. As a consequence, a package with a <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> can be used for the construction of a group of related subprograms in which the logical operations available to clients are clearly isolated from the internal entities.] <br /></p>

#### Legality Rules

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
4/5
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<p>{"{"}<em>{"AI12-0417-1"}</em>{"}"} <br />A <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> shall be the completion of a previous <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.1#S0229">package_declaration</a></code> or <code><a href="../AA-12/AA-12.1#S0312">generic_package_declaration</a></code>. A library <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.1#S0229">package_declaration</a></code> or library <code><a href="../AA-12/AA-12.1#S0312">generic_package_declaration</a></code> shall not have a body unless it requires a body[; the Elaborate_Body aspect can be used to require a <code><a href="../AA-10/AA-10.1#S0288">library_unit_declaration</a></code> to have a body (see <a href="../AA-10/AA-10.2#Subclause_10.2.1">10.2.1</a>) if it would not otherwise require one]. <br /></p>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
4.a
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="ramification" title="Ramification: ">
<strong></strong>The first part of the rule forbids a <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> from standing alone - it has to belong to some previous <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.1#S0229">package_declaration</a></code> or <code><a href="../AA-12/AA-12.1#S0312">generic_package_declaration</a></code>.<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
4.b
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
A nonlibrary <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.1#S0229">package_declaration</a></code> or nonlibrary <code><a href="../AA-12/AA-12.1#S0312">generic_package_declaration</a></code> that does not require a completion may have a corresponding body anyway. <br /></Admonition>


#### Static Semantics

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
5/3
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<p>{"{"}<em>{"AI05-0299-1"}</em>{"}"} <br />In any <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> without <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0167">statement</a></code>s there is an implicit <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0170">null_statement</a></code>. For any <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.1#S0229">package_declaration</a></code> without an explicit completion, there is an implicit <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> containing a single <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.1#S0170">null_statement</a></code>. For a noninstance, nonlibrary package, this body occurs at the end of the <code><a href="../AA-3/AA-3.11#S0086">declarative_part</a></code> of the innermost enclosing program unit or <code><a href="../AA-5/AA-5.6#S0191">block_statement</a></code>; if there are several such packages, the order of the implicit <code>package_bodies</code> is unspecified. [(For an instance, the implicit <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> occurs at the place of the instantiation (see <a href="../AA-12/AA-12.3">12.3</a>). For a library package, the place is partially determined by the elaboration dependences (see Clause <a href="../AA-10/">10</a>).)] <br /></p>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
5.a
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="discussion" title="Discussion: ">
<strong></strong>Thus, for example, we can refer to something happening just after the <strong>begin</strong> of a <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code>, and we can refer to the <code><a href="../AA-11/AA-11.2#S0304">handled_sequence_of_statements</a></code> of a <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code>, without worrying about all the optional pieces. The place of the implicit body makes a difference for tasks activated by the package. See also RM83-9.3(5).<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
5.b
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
The implicit body would be illegal if explicit in the case of a library package that does not require (and therefore does not allow) a body. This is a bit strange, but not harmful. <br /></Admonition>


#### Dynamic Semantics

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
6
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<p>For the elaboration of a nongeneric <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code>, its <code><a href="../AA-3/AA-3.11#S0086">declarative_part</a></code> is first elaborated, and its <code><a href="../AA-11/AA-11.2#S0304">handled_sequence_of_statements</a></code> is then executed. <br /></p>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
7
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
NOTE 1   A variable declared in the body of a package is only visible within this body and, consequently, its value can only be changed within the <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code>. In the absence of local tasks, the value of such a variable remains unchanged between calls issued from outside the package to subprograms declared in the visible part. The properties of such a variable are similar to those of a "static" variable of C.<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
8
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
NOTE 2   The elaboration of the body of a subprogram explicitly declared in the visible part of a package is caused by the elaboration of the body of the package. Hence a call of such a subprogram by an outside program unit raises the exception Program_Error if the call takes place before the elaboration of the <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> (see <a href="../AA-3/AA-3.11">3.11</a>). <br /></Admonition>

#### Wording Changes from Ada 83

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
15.a
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
The syntax rule for <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> now uses the syntactic category <code><a href="../AA-11/AA-11.2#S0304">handled_sequence_of_statements</a></code>.<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
15.b
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
The <code><a href="../AA-3/AA-3.11#S0086">declarative_part</a></code> of a <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> is now required; that doesn't make any real difference, since a <code><a href="../AA-3/AA-3.11#S0086">declarative_part</a></code> can be empty.<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
15.c
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
RM83 seems to have forgotten to say that a <code><a href="../AA-7/AA-7.2#S0231">package_body</a></code> can't stand alone, without a previous declaration. We state that rule here.<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
15.d
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
RM83 forgot to restrict the definition of elaboration of <code>package_bodies</code> to nongeneric ones. We have corrected that omission.<br /></Admonition>

<AnnotatedOnly>
<MarginText>
15.e
</MarginText>
</AnnotatedOnly>
<Admonition type="aarm" aarm="note" title="Note: ">
The rule about implicit bodies (from RM83-9.3(5)) is moved here, since it is more generally applicable. <br /></Admonition>
